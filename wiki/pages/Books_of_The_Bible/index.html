Books of The Bible

The Bible is a collection of 66 books written over a period of 1,500 years.

Old Testament

The Pentateuch

The Pentateuch, sometimes called the Five Books of Moses, tells the origins of the Jewish race. It may also be called the Torah, Hebrew for Law, as a large proportion of it consists of ancient Jewish law.

Gensis
Gensis means the beginnings. It tells the story of God's creation of the heavens and earth, Adam and Eve, The Flood, Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob and his sons.
